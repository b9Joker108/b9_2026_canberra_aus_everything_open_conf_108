Dear Colleagues, Friends, and Visionaries,

We write to invite thoughtful collaboration on the formation of a global development platform anchored in a not-for-profit foundation that guides and governs a for-profit subsidiary, with sovereign local chapters operating internationally to advance shared humanitarian, educational, cultural, and social aims. This letter seeks to lay down a developmental scaffold and governance framework designed to balance mission integrity, fiduciary responsibility, and scalable impact across diverse jurisdictions.

- Our overarching purpose is to advance humane outcomes through principled engagement, capacity-building, and responsible resource stewardship that respects local sovereignty and global accountability.

- Programmatic development: A modular framework that allows local chapters to design initiatives that reflect local needs while contributing to global outcomes. Regular mechanism for knowledge transfer, capacity-building, and diaspora or community engagement.
- Financial stewardship: A dual-level financial architecture with separate ledgers for the foundation and the subsidiary, clear budgeting cycles, restricted and unrestricted funding governance, and stringent anti-fraud controls.

Legal and Compliance Considerations
- Legal structure and registrations: Compliance with jurisdictional requirements for not-for-profit entities and for-profit subsidiaries, including governance standards, reporting, and registration with relevant authorities.
- Cross-border compliance: Standards for international operations, including data protection, labor laws, tax considerations, anti-corruption measures, and sanctions screening.
- Risk management: A comprehensive risk framework addressing governance, financial, legal, operational, and reputational risks, with contingency and business continuity planning.

Ethical Commitment and Transparency
- Values: Integrity, accountability, inclusivity, respect for local sovereignty, and commitment to measurable positive impact.
- Transparency: Public disclosure of governance policies, annual reports, audited financials, and independent reviews. Stakeholder engagement channels shall be established to solicit feedback and accountability.

Engagement and Collaboration
- Call for participation: We invite non-profit leaders, legal scholars, human resources professionals, compliance specialists, donors, and public-interest collaborators to participate in the design, governance, and implementation process.
- Tender process: Formal solicitations for onboarding of early directors, counsel, and operational leadership will be issued, with explicit criteria, timelines, and evaluation methods.
- Capacity-building: Programs to transfer governance best practices, financial management, and legal literacy to local chapters, ensuring sustainable leadership pipelines.

Next Steps
- Establish a charter and initial governance framework, including a provisional organizational chart, core policies, and a phased implementation plan.
- Launch a transparent tender for core roles and an onboarding pathway for partner organizations and individuals with the necessary expertise.
- Initiate a pilot phase with a subset of local chapters to refine governance, reporting, and programmatic models before global expansion.
