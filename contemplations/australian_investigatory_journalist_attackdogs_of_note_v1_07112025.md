Here is a list of twenty notable Australian investigative journalists, in alphabetical order by family name, including their periods of influence, a brief framing paragraph, their magnum opus, and two additional notable works each:

| Name               | Dates Flourished  | Framing & Magnum Opus                               | Two Notable Works                              |
|--------------------|------------------|----------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Linton Besser      | 1990s–2020s      | Veteran investigative journalist for *The Age* and *SMH*. Magnum opus: exposés on political corruption. | "The Andrew Bolt Case" (2011), "Inside the PLP" (2020) |
| Bob Bottom         | 1970s–2000s      | Pioneered crime and corruption investigations, especially in Queensland. Magnum opus: Fitzgerald Inquiry reporting. | Reports on Queensland corruption (1987), "The Mafia in Australia" (2000) |
| Peter Charley      | 1980s–2010s      | Known for war and human rights investigations with CBS and ABC. Magnum opus: "The Killing Season" documentary series. | "Inside the Killing Season" (2015), "Warlords of Papua" (2009) |
| Neil Chenoweth     | 2010s–2020s      | Explosive business investigations for *AFR*, especially tax scandals. Magnum opus: PwC Tax Leak investigation (2023). | "Corporate Money Laundering" (2022), "Banking Scandal Exposé" (2021) |
| Ross Coulthart     | 1990s–2020s      | Broadcast and television investigative journalist with *60 Minutes*. Magnum opus: The Butcher of Bega medical scandal (2008). | IRA assassination reinvestigation (2010), Aboriginal Legal Services exposé (1996) |
| Carrie Fellner     | 2010s–2020s      | Investigative reporter focused on Indigenous affairs and social justice. Magnum opus: Paradise Poisoned investigation. | "Indigenous Deaths in Custody" (2018), "Water Crisis in Northern Territory" (2020) |
| Chris Masters      | 1980s–2010s      | Longstanding *Four Corners* reporter pursuing political and social justice investigations. Magnum opus: Fitzgerald Inquiry instigation (1987). | "The Moonlight State" (1987), "French Connections" Rainbow Warrior (1985) |
| Kate McClymont     | 1980s–2020s      | *Sydney Morning Herald* reporter renowned for in-depth political and crime exposés. Magnum opus: Reporting on NSW Police corruption. | "The Gang" (2003), "Crime and Corruption in NSW" (2018) |
| Nick McKenzie      | 2000s–2020s      | Award-winning journalist with *The Age/SMH*, famous for high-profile crime and political investigations. Magnum opus: Ben Roberts-Smith war crimes investigation (2018–2020). | "The Outlaw Economy" (2016), "The Crown Casino Scandal" (2023) |
| Pamela Williams    | 1990s–2020s      | Senior journalist *AFR*, known for business and financial investigations. Magnum opus: Corporate fraud exposures. | "Tax Manipulation" (2018), "Superannuation Scandals" (2015) |
| Andrew Fowler      | 1980s–2010s      | Groundbreaking documentary journalist on political reform and corruption. Magnum opus: "Four Corners" series on Australian politics. | "The Untold Story" (1993), "Political Scandals" (2000) |
| Mark Davis         | 1990s–2020s      | Investigations into environmental and Indigenous issues, known for *Foreign Correspondent*. Magnum opus: "Inside Australia's Prison System" (2019). | "Climate Crisis in Australia" (2015), "Indigenous Rights" (2017) |
| Paul Sakkal        | 2010s–2020s      | Political investigations for *The Age*, exposing misuse of public funds. Magnum opus: Daniel Andrews political investigations (2023). | "Victoria Police Scandal" (2022), "Public Housing Issues" (2020) |
| Helen Pitt        | 2000s–2020s      | Focused on health, social justice and Indigenous issues. Magnum opus: Exposés on child protection systems. | "Young Offenders Report" (2017), "Healthcare Failings" (2019) |
| Caroline Wilson    | 1980s–2020s      | Veteran sports investigative journalist at *The Age*. Magnum opus: Coverage combining sports and corruption. | "Inside AFL Politics" (2010), "Controversies in Sports" (2017) |
| Hedley Thomas      | 1990s–2020s      | Award-winning investigative journalist known for medical negligence and true crime podcasts. Magnum opus: *The Teacher's Pet* podcast series (2018). | "Sick to Death" (2007), "The Night Driver" (2020) |
| Rhett Wyman        | 2010s–2020s      | Investigative journalist with *SMH* focused on Indigenous affairs and social justice. Magnum opus: Reporting on the judiciary and custodial deaths. | "Indigenous Custodial Deaths" (2018), "Justice System Failures" (2019) |
| Edmund Tadros      | 2010s–2020s      | Business and investigative journalist at *AFR*, focusing on financial scandals. Magnum opus: PwC Tax Leak series (2023). | "Corporate Governance Failures" (2021), "Financial Industry Exposés" (2019) |
| Matt Peacock      | 1990s–2010s      | Former investigative journalist for *The Age*, known for exposés on environmental health issues. Magnum opus: Lead contamination scandal (2001). | "The Asbestos Files" (2006), "Industrial Pollution" (2008) |
| Julian Assange      | 2000s–2010s      | Founder of WikiLeaks, exposed classified information globally. Magnum opus: Iraq War logs and diplomatic cables (2010–2011). | "Collateral Murder" video release, Afghan War logs (2010) |

This list represents a mix of veterans and contemporary heavyweights in Australian investigative journalism with significant contributions to political, social, environmental, and criminal investigative reporting. Their work has had profound influence on Australian public life and policy reforms [1][2][3][4][5][6].
